THE DIGITAL MESH

“The first rule of any technology used in a business is that automation applied to an efficient operation will magnify the efficiency. The second is that automation applied to an inefficient operation will magnify the inefficiency.” These are the famous lines quoted by Bill Gates.

Today in the 21st century, many new technologies are emerging day by day. We might be already familiar with some concepts like cloud computing, big data, automation, advanced machine learning, internet of things and so on.

A digital mesh represents a blend of physical and virtual environments that carry the data generated in the form of text, voice, and video that is comprised of sensory and contextual data as well. A Digital Mesh produces, uses, and transmits information in different forms mentioned above to generate an ambient digital experience for the users.

A digital mesh uses various devices connected to each other including mobile, wearable, consumer, home, automotive, and other environmental devices that constitute the Internet of Things (IoT). As more and more objects continue to be IoT enabled, we continue to move towards a hyper connected environment where users would be able to communicate with all objects accessible by users at any time and from any place.

The digital mesh provides an ambient user experience. They are classified as: Device Mesh – This causes great interaction between devices, which results in significant development in augmented and virtual reality. 3D Printing Material – Blue print of every object is perfectly designed and obtained as 3D material. Biological 3D printing — such as the printing of skin and organs — is progressing from theory to reality. Smart Machines – Advanced machine learning is what makes smart machines appear intelligent, where autonomous agents like robots and virtual personal assistants become the main interface.

IT Reality: Adaptive Security and Advanced System Architecture – Information security is provided and threats are blocked in the business society. Systems built on GPUs and field-programmable gate arrays (FPGAs) will function more like human brains that are particularly suited to be applied to deep learning and other pattern-matching algorithms that smart machines use. These are used in cars, homes etc.

Mesh and Service Architecture (Internet of Things): It brings together many information sources, devices, apps, services and micro services into a flexible architecture in which apps extend across multiple endpoint devices and can coordinate with one another to produce a continuous digital experience. The technologies and standards in the IoT platform form a base set of capabilities for communicating, controlling, managing and securing endpoints in the IoT.

The digital mesh provides endless opportunities for various industries and practitioners alike. However new technologies continue to evolve and create answers to questions that haven’t been asked yet. As a result, increased investment in research and development has to be encouraged to generate more knowledge and theories which will help us understand the technology better.
